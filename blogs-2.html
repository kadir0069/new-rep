Understanding Your Blood Test Results

April 22, 2025 Dr. Michael Chen

1. Complete Blood Count (CBC)


The CBC provides information about the cells in your blood:


  • White Blood Cells (WBC): These fight infections. High levels might indicate an infection, inflammation, or immune system issue. Low levels could mean you're more vulnerable to illness.
  • Red Blood Cells (RBC): These carry oxygen. Too few may suggest anemia; too many might point to dehydration or heart disease.
  • Hemoglobin (Hgb): This protein carries oxygen. Low hemoglobin is another sign of anemia.
  • Hematocrit (Hct): The proportion of RBCs in your blood. Abnormal levels can suggest dehydration, anemia, or other blood disorders.
  • Platelets: Help with blood clotting. Low platelet count can lead to easy bruising or bleeding; high counts may increase clotting risks.

    • The goal is early detection and prevention of potential health issues before they become serious.



    2. Basic Metabolic Panel (BMP)


    The BMP measures different chemicals in the blood and can show how your body is functioning:

  • Glucose: Blood sugar. High levels may point to diabetes or prediabetes.
  • Calcium: Important for muscles, nerves, and bones. Abnormal levels can indicate kidney problems, bone disease, or hormone imbalances.
  • Electrolytes (Sodium, Potassium, Chloride, Bicarbonate): These control hydration, nerve function, and pH balance. Imbalances may reflect kidney issues, dehydration, or certain medications.
  • Blood Urea Nitrogen (BUN) & Creatinine: Indicators of kidney health. Elevated levels may signal kidney dysfunction or dehydration.

    • 3. Comprehensive Metabolic Panel (CMP)


    The CMP includes everything in the BMP, plus liver function tests:

  • ALT, AST: Enzymes found in the liver. Elevated levels may suggest liver inflammation or damage.
  • ALP: Can indicate liver or bone disorders.
  • Bilirubin: A waste product. High levels may suggest liver disease or bile duct issues.
  • Albumin & Total Protein: Low levels may reflect liver or kidney disease, or nutritional problems.

    • 4. Lipid Panel


    This test checks cholesterol and triglyceride levels, important for heart health:

  • Total Cholesterol: High levels increase the risk of heart disease.
  • HDL ("Good" Cholesterol): Helps remove bad cholesterol from your blood. Higher is better.
  • LDL ("Bad" Cholesterol): Can build up in arteries. Lower is better.
  • Triglycerides: Another type of fat in the blood. High levels may increase heart disease risk.


    • 5. Thyroid Function Tests


    These tests assess how well your thyroid is working:

  • TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone): High levels may indicate underactive thyroid (hypothyroidism); low levels may indicate overactive thyroid (hyperthyroidism).
  • T3 & T4: Hormones produced by the thyroid. Abnormal levels can help pinpoint the cause of thyroid issues.
